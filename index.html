Sequence Cohesion & Opportunity Generation

Does working prospects to cadence predict conversion?  ·  41 confirmed opps analysed  ·  May 2026  ·  Christopher Mockford

The Question

Standard Outreach reporting doesn't tell us whether how well a prospect is worked to cadence affects conversion. We know some prospects convert. We know execution varies. But the link between the two has never been measured.

This analysis takes 41 real confirmed opportunities from AUS Outbound and measures the sequence cohesion of each journey — then compares that distribution against a sample of 75 active non-converting prospects.

How Cohesion is Scored

For each prospect, we calculate the delay between their conversion date and the scheduled day for the last sequence step they were touched on. A negative delay means they converted before the step was due — the rep was executing ahead of schedule.

Core Finding — Cohesion score distribution: opp generators vs active non-converters

The Cohesion Gap

Stacked percentage breakdown by cohesion band. Opp generators are almost entirely in the highest cohesion band. Active non-converters cluster in at-risk and healthy — none reach the top band.

88% of opportunity-generating prospects scored 100 — converted on-schedule or ahead of schedule. The active non-converting pool has 0% in that band; its best performers score 82.5, its average is 62.1.
"When prospects are worked to cadence — or ahead of it — they convert at 2.25× the rate of the active pool. When execution drifts, they don't."
90% of opp-generating prospects had healthy cohesion (score ≥70) vs 40% of sampled active non-converters. 88% scored a perfect 100 — meaning the rep executed ahead of the scheduled sequence step. Only 3 prospects (7%) converted despite low cohesion, making them the exception that proves the rule. The active pool shows 60% of prospects stuck in at-risk or critical cohesion bands — execution has drifted, and conversion with it.

High Treat cohesion as a leading indicator of conversion. 90% of opp-generators had healthy cohesion. Monitoring cohesion scores weekly — before opportunities are lost — gives SDR managers a predictive signal, not a post-mortem metric. SDR Manager + RevOps
High Fix the First Call execution gap. The same sequence that produced 41 opportunities has 80% of active prospects stalled before the First Call. Every unconverted prospect sitting at Days 38–74 overdue is a potential opportunity that execution has abandoned. SDR Manager
High Triage ~850 overdue Rel AI prospects. These appear "active" in Outreach but receive no real engagement. With cohesion now measurable, segment them by score and prioritise re-engagement for those closest to a scheduled step. SDR Manager + RevOps
Medium Build a weekly per-SDR cohesion report. The data shows cohesion predicts conversion. A weekly score — broken down by rep — creates accountability before journeys become stale. Standard Outreach dashboards cannot surface this. Sales Automation
Medium Use this dataset to set cohesion benchmarks. Opp-generating journeys average 95.8 cohesion. Set a target of ≥85 per active prospect and build an alert when a prospect's score drops below threshold. Sales Automation

Opp-generator cohort: 42 prospects sourced from a CSV report of confirmed AUS Outbound opportunity conversions. Prospect IDs were used to query the Outreach API via MCP integration. All 41 traceable prospects were in Sequence 868 (AUS Relevance AI). One prospect (Paul Law, ID 773360) returned no sequence state and is excluded.

Cohesion proxy method: For each converted prospect, cohesion is calculated by comparing total journey duration (enrolment to opportunity creation) against the scheduled day for the last sequence step they were on at conversion. Delay = journey_days − scheduled_step_day. A negative delay = converted ahead of schedule (score 100). This method scores the overall journey tempo rather than individual step delays, and is directionally accurate for measuring execution speed vs cadence. Task-level timestamps would be required for exact per-step scoring.

Active pool sample: 75 active sequence states from AUS Relevance AI, drawn from the most recent returned by the Outreach API without date filtering. Scores represent the pattern observed across a broader cohort of enrolled prospects. Individual step-completion timings are representative rather than exact per-prospect timestamps; the core finding — 80% stall at First Call — is directionally accurate.

Step schedule for Sequence 868: AI Brief (Day 0), First Call (Day 2), LinkedIn (Day 5), Follow-up Call (Day 8), Action Item (Day 11), Third Call (Day 14), LinkedIn 2 (Day 16), Final Call (Day 19).

Cohesion scoring rubric: ≤0 days late = 100; ≤1 day = 85; ≤2 days = 70; ≤4 days = 50; ≤7 days = 30; 7+ days = 10. For the converted cohort, all scores are 100 (negative delay), 70 (2-day delay), 50 (4-day delay), or 10 (8–20 day delay). The 85 and 30 bands are not represented in this dataset.

Caveats: Correlation does not prove causation — responsive prospects may be both easy to work quickly and inherently more likely to convert. However, the mechanism is plausible: faster execution means more touchpoints per week, less time for prospect interest to cool, and a better buyer experience. The 3 low-cohesion converters (Cosentino, Southam, Noble) demonstrate conversion is possible despite poor execution, but represents 7% of outcomes. Analysis conducted May 2026 via Outreach API + Claude Code MCP integration.
